This section explores Python’s concurrency and parallelism paradigms, covering thread-based and process-based approaches to achieve efficient multi-tasking. It delves into threading mechanisms, context-local state management, and process isolation for CPU-bound workloads, while also examining high-level abstractions like `concurrent.futures` for parallel task execution. Additionally, it addresses inter-process communication (IPC), synchronization primitives, and thread-safe data structures to coordinate concurrent operations. The chapter concludes with a survey of Python’s standard library modules—such as `threading`, `multiprocessing`, and `asyncio`—tailored to different execution models, including I/O-bound, CPU-bound, and hybrid scenarios, with a focus on scalability, resource management, and task scheduling.
