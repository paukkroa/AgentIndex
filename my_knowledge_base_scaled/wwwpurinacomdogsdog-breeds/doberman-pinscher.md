# Doberman Pinscher

The **Doberman Pinscher** is a medium-to-large, high-energy breed known for its sleek, muscular build, intelligence, and loyalty. Standing at **24–28 inches tall** and weighing **65–100 lbs**, they possess a short, low-maintenance coat (black, blue, red, or fawn with rust markings) and require **daily vigorous exercise** to prevent boredom or destructive behavior. Originally bred in **19th-century Germany** by Louis Dobermann as guard dogs, they excel in **obedience, search/rescue, therapy work, and Schutzhund** competitions. While affectionate and trainable, their strong-willed nature demands **consistent, engaging training** and early socialization to ensure compatibility with families and other pets. Health concerns include **bloat (GDV), hip dysplasia, and heart conditions**, necessitating responsible breeding practices. With a **lifespan of 10–12 years**, they thrive on **high-protein diets** and regular veterinary care.
