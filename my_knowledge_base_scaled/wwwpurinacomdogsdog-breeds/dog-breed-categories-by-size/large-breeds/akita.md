# Akita

The **Akita** is a large, powerful Japanese breed (males: 26–28" / 75–130 lbs; females: 24–26" / 70–100 lbs) with a thick double coat, erect ears, and a curled tail, originally bred for hunting wild game like bears and boar. Known for its **loyalty, protectiveness, and moderate energy**, the Akita requires **daily exercise, mental stimulation, and early socialization** to curb aggression toward strangers or unfamiliar animals. Its **lifespan (10–13 years)** is impacted by potential health risks like **bloat, hip dysplasia, thyroid disorders, and eye issues**, mitigated by responsible breeding. Akitas shed minimally year-round but undergo **heavy seasonal molting**, necessitating **weekly brushing** (daily during shedding periods). Historically tied to Japan’s Imperial family, the breed nearly went extinct before revival efforts post-WWII.
