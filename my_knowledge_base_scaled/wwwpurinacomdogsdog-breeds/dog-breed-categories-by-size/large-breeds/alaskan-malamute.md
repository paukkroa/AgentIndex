# Alaskan Malamute

The **Alaskan Malamute** is one of the oldest Arctic sled dog breeds, originally bred in Alaska by the Mahlemut tribe for hauling heavy loads over long distances. Known for their **large size** (males: 25" tall, 85 lbs; females: 23" tall, 75 lbs), **medium-length double coat** (requiring daily brushing and occasional baths), and **medium energy**, they thrive in outdoor activities like sledding and agility. Their **color palette** ranges from light gray/black to sable/red or pure white. Highly intelligent yet strong-willed, Malamutes are **loyal, affectionate family companions** but demand **daily exercise** and mental stimulation. Historically named after the Indigenous Mahlemut people, they remain a majestic, athletic, and devoted breed ideal for active owners.
