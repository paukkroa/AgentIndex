# American Bulldog

The **American Bulldog** is a large, muscular breed (males: 75–100 lbs, females: 60–80 lbs) with a short, dense coat in white with brindle, brown, or tan markings. Descended from the English Bulldog, they were historically working dogs for herding and guarding. Known for their loyal, friendly, yet sometimes sensitive nature, they thrive with experienced owners in suburban/rural settings with ample outdoor space. Key care considerations include **large-breed nutrition** (high-protein, joint-supporting diets like Purina Pro Plan), **regular grooming** (especially facial wrinkles), and **moderate exercise** to prevent obesity. Health concerns include hip dysplasia, allergies, and cataracts, necessitating supplements (e.g., glucosamine) and routine veterinary care. Their goofy, goofy demeanor and alert barking make them unique companions.
