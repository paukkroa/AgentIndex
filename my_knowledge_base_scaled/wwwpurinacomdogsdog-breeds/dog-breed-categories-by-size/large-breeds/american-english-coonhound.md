# American English Coonhound

The **American English Coonhound** is a large, high-energy hound breed descended from **English Foxhounds** (Virginia Hounds), introduced to the U.S. in the 17th century by George Washington. Known for its **speed, endurance, and vocal "bawl"**—a persistent baying used to track prey—this breed excels in **hunting (raccoons/fox), field trials, tracking, and obedience**. Standing **23–27 inches tall** and weighing **50–75 lbs**, it features a **medium-length, low-maintenance coat** in colors like red-and-white ticked, blue-and-white ticked, or tricolor. Requiring **daily vigorous exercise**, the breed thrives on outdoor activities and social interaction with humans and other dogs. Its **hard, protective coat** demands minimal grooming but benefits from occasional brushing. Ideal for active owners, the American English Coonhound is a **vocal, sociable, and athletic** companion with a strong hunting heritage.
