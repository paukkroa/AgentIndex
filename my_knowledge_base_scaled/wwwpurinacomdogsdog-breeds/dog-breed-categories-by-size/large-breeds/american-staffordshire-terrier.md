# American Staffordshire Terrier

The **American Staffordshire Terrier** is a large, muscular breed (males: 18–19" / 55–70 lbs; females: 17–18" / 40–55 lbs) with a short, low-maintenance coat and a powerful, agile build. Known for its **friendly, intelligent, and loyal temperament**, this people-oriented breed thrives on mental and physical challenges, excelling in activities like agility, obedience, and tracking. Descended from British bulldog and terrier lines, it was originally bred for strength and endurance before evolving into a family companion. With a **lifespan of 12–16 years**, AmStaffs require regular exercise, quality nutrition (adult or weight-management food for adults, puppy food for the first year), and occasional brushing. While generally healthy, responsible breeders screen for genetic issues like hip dysplasia and allergies. Historically notable for roles in early auto trips, film (e.g., *Our Gang*), and even WWI (Sergeant Stubby), this breed balances strength with affection, though its strong-willed nature demands consistent training.
