# Bulldog

The **Bulldog** is a medium-sized, low-energy breed (13–15 inches tall, 40–50+ lbs) known for its short coat, distinctive wrinkles, and affectionate yet courageous temperament. Originally bred in 13th-century England for bullbaiting—a now-banned brutal sport—the breed was later reimagined as a loyal companion after the sport’s ban in 1835. Bulldogs thrive as family pets, offering minimal grooming needs (weekly brushing to manage shedding and wrinkle hygiene) but require moderate exercise (e.g., walks) to avoid obesity. Their lifespan averages **8–10 years**, with common health concerns including overheating and breed-specific dietary needs. Nutritionally, they benefit from high-quality dog foods tailored to their size (e.g., **Pro Plan, Purina ONE, or Beneful**) and puppies require **DHA-rich formulas** for brain development. Coat colors range from red/fawn to brindle/white, often with black masks or ticking. Historically, they symbolize England’s tenacity, embodying a mix of resilience and gentle devotion.
