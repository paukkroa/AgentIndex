The **Hunting & Scent-Based Breeds** section explores iconic canine breeds developed for tracking, trailing, and scent detection, blending historical purpose with distinctive physical and behavioral traits. From the **American Foxhound**, a medium-to-large, high-energy hound with a short coat and keen nose, to the **Basset Hound**, celebrated for its low-energy, droopy-eared build and soulful expression, these breeds showcase diverse adaptations for scent work. The **Beagle**, a compact yet sturdy scent-hound with a legendary history as a hunting companion, exemplifies the breed’s balance of agility, endurance, and vocalization. Together, these breeds highlight the evolution of scent-based working dogs, their unique anatomical features, and their enduring roles in hunting, detection, and companionship.
