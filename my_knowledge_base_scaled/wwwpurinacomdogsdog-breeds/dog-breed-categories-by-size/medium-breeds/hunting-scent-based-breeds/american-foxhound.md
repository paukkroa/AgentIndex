# American Foxhound

The **American Foxhound**, a rare and historic breed developed by George Washington in the 1700s, is a medium-to-large, short-coated hound known for its speed, endurance, and adaptability to rural or active household environments. Standing **21–25 inches tall** and weighing **40–65 lbs**, this breed excels in field trials, hunting, and night hunts, though its stubborn yet mild temperament requires early socialization and training. With **medium energy**, it thrives in outdoor activities but can adapt to smaller homes if exercised sufficiently. Friendly with children and most pets, the American Foxhound is also the **official state dog of Virginia**, though its independent nature demands consistent leadership. Recommended products include **Pro Plan formulas** for digestion and skin health, and it shares traits with other hounds, medium-energy breeds, and short-haired dogs.
