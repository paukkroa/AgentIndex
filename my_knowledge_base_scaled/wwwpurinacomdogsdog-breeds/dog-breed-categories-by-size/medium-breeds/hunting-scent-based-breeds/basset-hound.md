# Basset Hound

The **Basset Hound** is a medium-sized, low-energy scenthound known for its distinctive short legs, droopy ears, and soulful eyes, originally bred in France and Belgium as a tracking and hunting companion for small to medium game like rabbits, deer, and foxes. Despite its compact stature (up to 15 inches tall and 45–65 lbs), the breed exhibits surprising stamina and strength. While affectionate, patient, and child-friendly, Bassets are stubborn and strong-willed, requiring **early, consistent training and socialization** to manage. Their short coat requires **weekly grooming** to control moderate shedding, and they are prone to health issues like **obesity, ear infections, hip dysplasia, and hypothyroidism**. Lifespan averages **12–13 years**, with a diet of **high-quality adult or puppy food** (depending on age) essential for their well-being. Historically tied to French aristocracy, Bassets were gifted to notable figures like George Washington. Their unique genetic origins (possibly from dwarfed French hunting hounds) and cultural significance—including a *Time* magazine feature—add to their charm.
