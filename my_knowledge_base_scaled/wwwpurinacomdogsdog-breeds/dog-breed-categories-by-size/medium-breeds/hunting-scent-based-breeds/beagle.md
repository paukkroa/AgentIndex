# Beagle

The **Beagle** is a small to medium-sized (13–15" tall, 18–35 lbs) scent-hound breed with a rich history as a hunting companion, originally bred by English gentlemen in the 1500s for pack work. Known for their **affectionate, intelligent, and energetic** temperament, Beagles thrive in social settings, requiring companionship to avoid restlessness or destructive behavior. Their **tri-color (black, tan, white) or varied coat patterns** (including red, lemon, blue, etc.) complement their **dense double coat**, which sheds seasonally and year-round. As **highly scent-driven trackers**, they excel in agility, obedience, and field trials but need **secure leashes/walls** due to escape tendencies. Health-wise, responsible breeders screen for **hip dysplasia, hypothyroidism, epilepsy, and eye disorders**, with a **lifespan of 10–15 years**.
