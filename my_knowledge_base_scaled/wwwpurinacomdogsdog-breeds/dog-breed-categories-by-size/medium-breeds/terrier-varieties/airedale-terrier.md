# Airedale Terrier

The **Airedale Terrier**, often called the "King of Terriers," is a medium-sized, high-energy breed originating from England’s Valley of Aire, bred by working-class mill workers for hunting, guarding, and companionship. Standing **23 inches tall** and weighing **50–70 lbs**, this sturdy breed features a **dense, wiry black-and-tan or grizzle-and-tan coat**, alert expression, and a confident, regal gait. Known for their **bold yet patient temperament**, Airedales excel in **police work, agility, obedience, hunting, and family life**, thriving as active, loyal companions. With a **lifespan of 11–14 years**, they require **weekly brushing** and **seasonal grooming** to manage shedding. Generally healthy but prone to **hip dysplasia**, they benefit from **high-quality adult or puppy food** tailored to their needs. Historically versatile, Airedales served in **World War I, hunting, and police work**, and were favored by presidents like Woodrow Wilson. Their **intelligence, athleticism, and adaptability** make them a standout breed in both work and home settings.
