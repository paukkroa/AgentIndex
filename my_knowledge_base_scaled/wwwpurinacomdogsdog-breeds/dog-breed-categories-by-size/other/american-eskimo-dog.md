# American Eskimo Dog

The **American Eskimo Dog** (*"Eskie"*) is a medium-energy, intelligent, and affectionate breed descended from European spitz-type dogs, introduced to the U.S. by German immigrants. Known for its **three size varieties**—Toy (9–10"), Miniature (12–15"), and Standard (15–19")—this breed weighs **6–40 lbs** and sports a **fluffy double coat** in white or cream-white hues. Historically, Eskies were **circus performers**, with one even becoming the first dog to walk a tightrope. Highly trainable and agile, they excel in **conformation, agility, and obedience** competitions. However, their **skeptical nature toward strangers** and **weekly grooming needs** (to maintain coat health) require attentive care. Their playful yet intelligent temperament makes them adaptable companions, though their vocal tendencies may suit active households.
