# Bull Terrier

The **Bull Terrier** is a medium-to-large, muscular breed (21–22" tall, 50–70 lbs) with a short coat in any color or all-white, known for its egg-shaped head, playful personality, and medium energy. Originally bred from Bulldogs and Terriers in 19th-century England for dogfighting (after bull-baiting was banned), this breed evolved into a spirited, affectionate companion. Key traits include high intelligence (requiring firm, early training), loyalty, and adaptability to activities like agility and obedience. Health concerns may include kidney/heart issues and deafness, but responsible breeders mitigate risks. Lifespan averages **12–13 years**. Their low-shedding coat needs weekly brushing, and they thrive on socialization, exercise, and a calcium-rich diet (especially for puppies). Historically, they transitioned from underground fighting dogs to beloved pets, celebrated by figures like President Theodore Roosevelt and Hollywood stars.
