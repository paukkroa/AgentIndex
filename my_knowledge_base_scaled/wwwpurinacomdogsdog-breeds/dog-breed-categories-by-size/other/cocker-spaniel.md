# Cocker Spaniel

The **Cocker Spaniel** is a small, medium-energy sporting breed (14.5–15.5" males, 20–30 lbs) with a long, silky coat and gentle, affectionate temperament, making it an ideal companion for families. Bred for hunting woodcock, it excels in agility, obedience, and field trials. Key traits include intelligence, sociability, and a need for daily exercise. Health considerations include obesity risk, hip/eye disorders, and ear infections due to its long ears. Lifespan ranges from **10–14 years**, with low shedding but high grooming needs (daily brushing, monthly trims). Historically rooted in Spain, the breed was later standardized in the 19th century, with American and English varieties recognized separately. Notable figures like Presidents Hayes and Nixon owned Cocker Spaniels, and the breed gained fame through *Lady and the Tramp*.
