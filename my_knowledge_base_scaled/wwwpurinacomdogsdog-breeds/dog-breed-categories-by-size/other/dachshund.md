# Dachshund

The **Dachshund**, or "wiener dog," is a small yet bold German breed (12–16 years lifespan) with a distinctive elongated body and short legs, available in **Standard (8–9" tall, 16–32 lbs)** or **Miniature (5–6" tall, up to 11 lbs)** sizes. Known for their **medium energy, independent yet loyal temperament**, and **moderate shedding** (with longhaired varieties requiring more grooming), Dachshunds excel in activities like agility, tracking, and obedience. Their **coat types**—short, long, or wirehaired—come in diverse colors (red, black & tan, brindle, dapple, etc.). While generally healthy, they are prone to **back injuries** (due to their low-slung physique) and ear infections (from droopy ears). Training requires **patience and positive reinforcement** to manage their stubborn streak. Diet-wise, **small-breed or weight-management formulas** are ideal to prevent obesity, while **puppy-specific foods** (with DHA/antioxidants) support growth.
