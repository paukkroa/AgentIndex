# Havanese

The **Havanese** is a small, sturdy toy breed originating from Havana, Cuba, known for its cheerful, affectionate, and intelligent temperament. Standing **8.5–11.5 inches** tall and weighing **7–13 lbs**, this breed features a **long, hypoallergenic double coat** (sometimes corded with care) and moderate energy, excelling in activities like agility, obedience, and rally. Ideal for families, they bond well with children and pets but retain a watchful nature. With a **lifespan of 14–16 years**, they require **daily moderate exercise** and **regular grooming** to prevent tangles. Prone to minor health issues like heart murmurs or hip problems, responsible breeders screen for genetic conditions. Their diet benefits from **small-breed kibble** for easy chewing and proper nutrition. Descended from Bichon breeds, the Havanese was once a pampered companion to Cuban aristocrats before gaining global popularity after Cuban exiles brought them to the U.S. Notable for their versatility—from lapdog to watchdog—they’re celebrated for their adaptability and historical ties to writers like Dickens and Hemingway
