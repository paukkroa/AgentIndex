# Skye Terrier

The **Skye Terrier** is a rare, medium-sized breed originating from the Isle of Skye (Scotland) in the 1600s, known for its distinctive short legs, large head, and long, soft coat. Standing at **9.5–10 inches tall** (females/males) and weighing **25–40 lbs**, this breed exhibits **medium energy**, thriving in activities like agility, obedience, and pet therapy. Their **affectionate yet cautious** temperament makes them loyal family companions, though they may be reserved with strangers. Grooming demands regular **weekly brushing and trimming** due to their long coat, while their **medium energy** requires consistent exercise. Historically notable for loyalty (e.g., "Greyfriars Bobby"), Skye Terriers are one of the oldest terrier breeds, prized for their **friendly yet dignified** demeanor.
