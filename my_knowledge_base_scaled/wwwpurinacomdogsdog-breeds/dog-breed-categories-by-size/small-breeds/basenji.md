# Basenji

The **Basenji**, an ancient African hunting breed, is a small, elegant, and high-energy dog known as the "Barkless Dog" due to its unique yodel-like vocalization instead of barking. Males stand at least **17 inches** (22–26 lbs) and females at least **15 inches** (20–24 lbs), with a short, odor-free coat in colors like chestnut red, black, tricolor, or brindle, often marked with white on feet, chest, and tail tip. Renowned for its aloofness with strangers yet affectionate, intelligent, and playful nature, the Basenji thrives in activities such as agility, hunting, and obedience. As one of the oldest breeds, it was historically gifted to Egyptian pharaohs. Its minimal grooming needs and low-shedding coat make it ideal for indoor living, though its high energy demands consistent exercise and mental stimulation.
