# Dandie Dinmont Terrier

The **Dandie Dinmont Terrier**, a small yet sturdy breed (8–11" tall, 18–24 lbs) with a distinctive **medium-length, double-coated** appearance in **pepper or mustard colors**, originated in 18th-century England/Scotland as a **hunting and badger/otter-tracking dog**. Known for its **medium energy**, this breed excels in **hunting, earthdog trials, and conformation shows** but is now celebrated as a **loyal, affectionate companion**, particularly with children. While protective and alert, its **terrier temperament** may include **dominance toward other dogs/animals**. Grooming requires **professional trimming or hand-stripping** to maintain its signature coat. Historically favored by **Queen Victoria and gypsies**, the breed’s name derives from Sir Walter Scott’s novel *Guy Mannering*. Recommended for owners seeking a **low-shedding, medium-energy** dog with a **distinctive appearance and strong personality**.
