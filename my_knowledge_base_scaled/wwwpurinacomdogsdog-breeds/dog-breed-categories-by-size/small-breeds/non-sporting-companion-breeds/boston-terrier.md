# Boston Terrier

The **Boston Terrier**, affectionately known as *"The American Gentleman,"* is a small, medium-energy companion breed (15–17" tall, 15–25 lbs) with a short, low-shedding coat and classic black-and-white (or brindle/seal) markings. Renowned for their friendly, intelligent, and amusing temperament, they thrive as urban pets due to their adaptability and affectionate nature. While some enjoy vigorous play or canine sports (agility, obedience, flyball), they generally require moderate exercise and mental stimulation to prevent boredom. Key health considerations include **brachycephalic breathing challenges**, **eye issues** (daily checks recommended), and **patellar luxation**. Nutrition should prioritize **small-breed formulas** for adults and **DHA-rich puppy foods** for optimal growth. Historically derived from 19th-century English Bulldog-White English Terrier crosses, the breed evolved from pit fighters into the refined, people-oriented dogs recognized today.
