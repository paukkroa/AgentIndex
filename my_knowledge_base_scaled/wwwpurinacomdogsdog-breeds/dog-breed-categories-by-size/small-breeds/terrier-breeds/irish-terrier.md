# Irish Terrier

The **Irish Terrier** is a medium-sized, high-energy breed known for its **bright red, wiry, low-shedding coat** and **compact yet athletic build** (18" tall, 25–27 lbs). Originally bred for **vermin control** on farms, this breed exhibits **bold courage, loyalty, and high prey drive**, making it well-suited for **hunting, agility, police/military work, and competitive dog sports** (conformation, rally, tracking). With a **medium energy level**, it thrives on **daily exercise, fenced outdoor access, and mental stimulation**. Historically descended from **ancient British and Irish terriers**, the breed was formally recognized in **1875** and played roles as a **World War I messenger and sentinel**. Its **affectionate yet territorial nature** (dominant with other dogs) requires early socialization. Recommended for active owners, it pairs well with **high-protein diets** (e.g., Pro Plan Puppy formulas) to support its active lifestyle.
