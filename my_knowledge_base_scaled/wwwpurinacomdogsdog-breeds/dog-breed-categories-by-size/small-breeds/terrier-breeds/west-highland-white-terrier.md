# West Highland White Terrier

The **West Highland White Terrier** ("Westie") is a small, medium-energy companion breed known for its bright white double coat, compact build, and intelligent yet independent temperament. Standing at **10–11 inches tall** and weighing **13–22 lbs**, Westies require **daily exercise** (leashed walks or fenced play) and **consistent training** due to their strong-willed nature. Their **rough, low-shedding coat** demands **daily brushing** and **monthly trims** to maintain health. Prone to **weight gain, cardiac disease, and luxating patella**, responsible breeders screen for genetic conditions. Originally bred in **17th-century Scotland** as rodent hunters, Westies gained popularity at dog shows in the late 1800s and were AKC-recognized in **1908**. With a **lifespan of 13–15 years**, they thrive on **small-breed-specific food** and thrive as loyal, affectionate family pets.
