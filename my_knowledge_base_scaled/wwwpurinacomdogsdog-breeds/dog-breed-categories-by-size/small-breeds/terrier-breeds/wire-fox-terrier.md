# Wire Fox Terrier

The **Wire Fox Terrier** is a small, sturdy, and energetic breed originating in 17th-century Britain, originally bred for fox hunting. Known for its **medium energy, bold temperament, and playful personality**, this terrier excels in agility and conformation competitions while serving as an alert watchdog. With a **short, wiry coat** (typically white with black/tan markings), it requires **regular brushing and occasional hand-stripping** for show dogs or low-maintenance clipping for pets. Generally healthy, responsible breeders screen for conditions like **eye disorders, hip dysplasia, and wobbler’s gait**. Historically significant—from King Edward VII’s beloved Caesar to Hollywood’s Asta—the breed was recognized separately from Smooth Fox Terriers by the AKC in **1985**. Lifespan ranges from **12–15 years**, and its small size (males ≤19 lbs, females ≤17 lbs) suits specialized **small-breed nutrition**, including high-quality puppy food for growth.
