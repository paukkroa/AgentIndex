# Affenpinscher

The **Affenpinscher** is a small, ancient toy breed (9–11.5" tall, 7–10 lbs) known for its bold, playful, and intelligent personality despite its diminutive size. Originating in 17th-century Germany as a stable pest-control dog, it later evolved into a hypoallergenic companion with a wiry, medium-length coat (requiring 2–3 weekly brushings). While sturdy and compact with a square build, its medium energy demands moderate indoor play and outdoor exercise. Though affectionate and loyal, it lacks tolerance for rough handling (e.g., hugging/chasing) and may clash with small pets like rodents. Health-wise, it’s generally robust but prone to seasonal shedding, brachycephalic breathing issues (heat sensitivity), weight gain, and conditions like hip dysplasia or patellar luxation. Lifespan ranges from **12–15 years**, with coat colors spanning black, gray, silver, red, tan, and beige.
