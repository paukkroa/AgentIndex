# Bolognese

The **Bolognese**, a small (6–10 lbs), low-energy toy breed with a distinctive **pure white, curly/wavy coat** (or silkier tail/ear hair), traces its origins to 18th-century Italian nobility, favored by figures like Madame Pompadour and Catherine the Great. Known for their **high-maintenance grooming needs** (frequent brushing) and **vocal nature** (alert barking), Bolognese thrive as affectionate lap dogs in small spaces. Their **low exercise requirements** and trainability (including trick training) make them ideal for indoor living. Nutrition demands **small-breed-specific formulas** (e.g., Purina Pro Plan Toy Breed) to prevent obesity, with kibble/wet food adjustments for weight management. While generally healthy, their **chatty personality** and **delicate size** necessitate careful feeding (2+ meals/day) and **low-calorie treats** to sustain their lively yet calm demeanor.
