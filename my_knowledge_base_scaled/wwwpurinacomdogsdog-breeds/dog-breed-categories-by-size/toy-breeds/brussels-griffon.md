# Brussels Griffon

The **Brussels Griffon** is a compact, toy-sized Belgian breed (7–8 inches tall, 6–12 lbs) renowned for its expressive, almost human-like face—marked by large eyes, short muzzles, and scruffy beards/mustaches. With medium energy, they thrive as indoor companions, requiring daily walks but adaptable to apartment living. Their coats come in **red, black, black-and-tan, or belge**, with two varieties: **smooth-coated** (seasonal shedding, needs daily brushing) or **rough-coated** (low-shedding, clipped short). Known for intelligence, sensitivity, and loyalty, they excel in obedience and conformation shows. Health-wise, they’re generally robust but prone to **breathing issues (due to brachycephalic traits), heart conditions, cataracts, and hip dysplasia**. Lifespan averages **12–15 years**. Originating in 19th-century Brussels as rat-controlling stable dogs (*griffons d’écurie*), their lineage likely includes **Pugs, Affenpinschers, and Yorkshire Terriers**, elevated to royal status by Queen Henrietta Maria of Belgium.
