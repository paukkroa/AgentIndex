# Yorkshire Terrier

The **Yorkshire Terrier**, a small yet spirited **toy breed**, stands out with its **luxurious floor-length coat**, typically in **blue-and-tan or black-and-gold hues**, and a compact, sturdy build (6–9 inches tall, up to 7 lbs). Known for their **bold, protective, and intelligent temperament**, Yorkies thrive as **watchdogs** and require **early training and socialization** to curb stubbornness. Their **high energy** demands short daily walks and mental stimulation (e.g., agility, obedience). As **hypoallergenic** but **low-shedding** dogs, they need **daily brushing and weekly baths** to prevent tangles. Health-wise, they’re generally robust but prone to **eye issues and luxating patella** (mitigated by limiting jumps). Historically bred by **Scottish weavers** in the 19th century to eradicate pests in tight spaces, Yorkies later became **fashionable lapdogs**, evolving into smaller sizes. Their **lifespan (11–15 years)** and adaptability make them ideal companions for active owners willing to invest in grooming and training
