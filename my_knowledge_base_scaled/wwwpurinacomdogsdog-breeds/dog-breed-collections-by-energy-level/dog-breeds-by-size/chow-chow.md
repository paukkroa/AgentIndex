# Chow Chow

The **Chow Chow** is an ancient, medium-to-large breed originating from northern China, known for its regal appearance, muscular build, and distinctive features such as a lion-like mane, blue-black tongue, and deep-set almond eyes. Standing at **18–22 inches tall** and weighing **44–70 lbs**, this breed exhibits a **medium-energy temperament**, requiring moderate exercise like walks or play. While affectionate and loyal to their families, Chows can be **stubborn and aloof with strangers**, necessitating **early socialization and consistent training**. Their **thick double coat**—either rough or smooth—demands **regular grooming**, especially during seasonal shedding. Health concerns may include **hip/elbow dysplasia, eye issues, and thyroid disorders**, so responsible breeding is advised. Historically used as **guardians, hunters, and companions**, Chows gained global recognition in the 19th century after Queen Victoria’s interest. They thrive on **balanced wet or dry dog food**, with puppies needing specialized nutrition for growth. Notable traits include **low odor, easy housetraining, and a lifespan of 8–12 years**.
