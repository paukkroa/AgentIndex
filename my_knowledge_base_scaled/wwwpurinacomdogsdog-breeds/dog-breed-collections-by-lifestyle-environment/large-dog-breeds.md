# Large Dog Breeds

Large dog breeds encompass a diverse range of temperaments, from high-energy, athletic companions like the **Afghan Hound** (known for speed and endurance) and **Belgian Malinois** (intelligent, agile working dogs) to more laid-back, affectionate breeds such as the **Alaskan Malamute** (friendly yet sturdy) and **Bearded Collie** (playful yet adaptable). These breeds vary in origin—herding (e.g., **Belgian Sheepdog**), hunting (e.g., **American Foxhound**), or companionship (e.g., **Beauceron**)—with distinct traits like size (often 50+ lbs), coat types (long-haired Afghan Hounds vs. short-coated Akitas), and exercise needs (moderate to high). Ideal for active owners or those seeking loyal, protective, or cuddly partners, these breeds require tailored care, from grooming (e.g., **Bergamasco’s** curly coat) to mental stimulation (e.g., **Malinois’** training demands).
