# Curly-Coated Retriever

The **Curly-Coated Retriever** is a large, high-energy sporting breed known for its distinctive **curly coat** (black or liver) and athletic build, standing **23–27 inches tall** and weighing **65 lbs**. Descended from 16th-century English retrievers, this breed excels in **hunting, agility, obedience, and field trials**, thriving on vigorous daily exercise to prevent boredom or destructive behavior. Renowned for its **gentle, family-friendly temperament**, it bonds closely with children and serves as a protective yet affectionate companion. Its **low-maintenance coat** requires minimal grooming, though occasional baths are recommended. Originally popularized in **New Zealand and Australia** after being imported in 1889, the breed was introduced to the U.S. in **1907**. Ideal for active owners, it pairs well with structured activities like swimming and training.
