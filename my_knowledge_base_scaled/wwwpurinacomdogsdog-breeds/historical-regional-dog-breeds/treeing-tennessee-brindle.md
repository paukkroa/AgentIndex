# Treeing Tennessee Brindle

The **Treeing Tennessee Brindle** is a medium-sized, medium-energy hunting breed developed in the 1960s by Reverend Earl Phillips, closely related to Mountain Curs and Plott Hounds. Known for their distinctive **brindle or black coat with white markings**, these dogs possess an exceptional **sense of smell**, a **strong, clear bay (singsong vocalization)**, and a natural instinct to **"tree"** (chase and bark at small game until it climbs a tree). Hardy, intelligent, and loyal, they thrive in active outdoor lifestyles, excelling in activities like hiking, camping, and kayaking. Their care requires **high-protein, balanced nutrition** (ideal for active dogs), **regular vet check-ups** (though generally healthy), and **moderate grooming** (low-maintenance short coat). Social, courageous yet easygoing, they bond well with families but need consistent engagement to channel their energy.
