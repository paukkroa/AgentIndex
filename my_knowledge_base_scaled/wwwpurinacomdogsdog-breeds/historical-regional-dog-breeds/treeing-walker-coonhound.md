# Treeing Walker Coonhound

The **Treeing Walker Coonhound** is a large, high-energy hound breed (20–27" tall, 50–70 lbs) with a short, glossy coat in tricolored, black, or white. Descended from the English Foxhound, this breed was originally bred for tracking and "treing" (flushing out) raccoons, showcasing exceptional speed, stamina, and a keen nose. Known for its bold, confident hunting instincts, it also thrives as an affectionate family companion. Requires **daily vigorous exercise** and minimal grooming. Popular activities include hunting, agility, obedience, and field trials. Historically recognized as a distinct breed in **1945**, it remains a vocal, athletic, and loyal hound.
