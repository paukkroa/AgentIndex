# Bullmastiff

The **Bullmastiff** is a **large/giant breed** (males: 25–27 in, 110–130 lbs; females: 24–26 in, 100–120 lbs) with a **short, low-maintenance coat** in colors like red, fawn, or brindle. Originating in **19th-century England**, this breed was bred as a **guardian** for estates, known for its **confident yet docile temperament**, making it an excellent **family companion**. Bullmastiffs exhibit **low energy**, requiring minimal exercise and grooming, and are **quiet protectors**—ideal for apartments or homes. They excel in activities like **agility, obedience, and tracking** and are part of **guard dog and working breed categories**. Historically, they were bred to **detect and deter poachers** without excessive barking. Notable for their **calm demeanor** and **versatile adaptability**, they balance strength with gentle nature.
