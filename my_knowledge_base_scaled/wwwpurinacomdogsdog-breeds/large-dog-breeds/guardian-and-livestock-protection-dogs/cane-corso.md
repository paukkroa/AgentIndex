# Cane Corso

The **Cane Corso** is a powerful, large working breed (molosser type) with a noble, protective temperament, originally bred as a property watchdog and large-game hunter. Standing **23.5–27.5 inches tall** and weighing **88–110 lbs**, this breed features a **short, double-layered coat** in colors like black, brindle, fawn, or red, often with a mask. Known for intelligence, loyalty, and affection, Corsi thrive on structured activities like agility, obedience, and protection sports but require **daily vigorous exercise** (e.g., brisk walks or jogs) to stay healthy. Early socialization is critical to curb protective instincts and ensure compatibility with children and other pets. Health concerns include **hip dysplasia, bloat, epilepsy, and skin conditions**, necessitating a **large-breed-specific diet** (e.g., Purina Pro Plan Giant Breed or Pro Plan SPORT for active dogs) and **weekly brushing** during shedding seasons. With a lifespan of **9–12 years**, Corsi excel as devoted family companions when properly trained and exercised.
