The **Herding and Farm Dogs** section explores ancient and modern breeds developed for livestock management, showcasing their unique traits, historical significance, and working capabilities. From the **Bergamasco**, an ancient Alpine herding dog with a distinctive hypoallergenic corded coat dating back over 2,000 years, to the **Berger Picard**, a medium-energy herder with a wiry coat, this section highlights breeds adapted to diverse climates and tasks. Each breed’s physical attributes, temperament, and historical context are examined to illustrate their enduring roles in agriculture and rural life.
