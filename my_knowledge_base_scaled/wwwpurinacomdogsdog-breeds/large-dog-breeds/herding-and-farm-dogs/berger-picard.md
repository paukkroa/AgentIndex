# Berger Picard

The **Berger Picard** is a large, sturdy French herding breed with a distinctive **short, wiry, weather-resistant coat** in **brindle or fawn hues**, requiring minimal grooming. Males stand **23.5–25.5 inches** tall, while females reach **21.5–23.5 inches**, weighing **50–75 lbs**. Known for their **medium energy**, they excel in **agility, obedience, herding, and tracking**, though their **stubborn yet loyal nature** demands a **patient, firm handler**. Historically used by French farmers since the **14th century**, their wiry coat once concealed **contraband pouches** during border crossings. Friendly and alert, they balance charm with a **lighthearted yet stubborn temperament**, making them ideal for active owners. Pronounced *"bare ZHAY pee CARR"*, this breed gained fame via the 2005 film *Because of Winn-Dixie* (portrayed by Picards "Scott" and "Lyco").
