# Bloodhound

The **Bloodhound** is a large, short-coated hound breed renowned for its **exceptional olfactory abilities**, capable of tracking scents from mere skin cells, making it indispensable in **police investigations, search-and-rescue missions, and criminal searches**. Standing **23–27 inches tall** and weighing **80–110 lbs**, Bloodhounds possess a distinctive **loose, wrinkled skin** and coat colors of **black-and-tan, liver-and-tan, or red**. With **low-to-medium energy**, they thrive as **affectionate yet independent** companions but require **regular grooming** (weekly brushing, ear cleaning, and baths) due to shedding and drooling. Health-wise, they are prone to **bloat and ear infections**, necessitating vigilant care. Historically bred by **European monasteries** for aristocratic hunting, their **AKC-recognized lineage** (since 1885) solidifies their reputation as **unmatched trackers**, with legal admissible evidence in courts. Ideal diets include **large-breed puppy/adult formulas** for proper growth and weight management.
