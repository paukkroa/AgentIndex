# Bouvier des Flandres

The **Bouvier des Flandres** is a large, rugged herding breed originating from Flanders, Belgium, historically bred for cattle herding, drafting, and guarding. Standing **23.5–27.5 inches** tall and weighing **65–100 lbs**, this medium-coated dog exhibits colors like fawn, black, salt-and-pepper, gray, or brindle. Known for its **high energy** and **calm, gentle temperament**, the Bouvier thrives as a family companion but requires **frequent grooming** to maintain its shaggy coat and **plenty of exercise**. Slow-maturing (reaching full adulthood at **2–3 years**), this versatile breed excels in **tracking, herding, police/military work, search/rescue, agility, and Schutzhund**. During **World War I**, Bouviers served as **message carriers and cart dogs**, transporting wounded soldiers. Ideal for active owners, they pair well with structured activities and moderate shedding maintenance.
