# Azawakh

The **Azawakh** is a medium-sized, high-energy African sighthound originating from West Africa (modern-day Mali and Niger), historically bred by Tuareg nomads as both a companion and hunter. Known for their sleek, aerodynamic build, they possess a short, fine coat in a variety of colors (fawn, brindle, black, white, etc.) and a lean, muscular physique optimized for speed and endurance. Independent yet graceful, Azawakhs thrive in active environments and require high-protein, low-carb diets to maintain their athletic, slender frame. Key care considerations include structured feeding (two meals/day) to prevent weight gain, low-calorie protein-rich treats, and routine veterinary care for preventive health (vaccinations, parasite control, and potential spay/neuter). Their rarity in the U.S. (introduced in the 1980s) and fierce independence necessitate experienced owners who can provide mental stimulation and exercise. Health-wise, they may benefit from joint supplements or omega-3s due to their active lifestyle.
