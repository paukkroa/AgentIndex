# Black and Tan Coonhound

The **Black and Tan Coonhound** is a medium-to-large, short-coated hunting breed known for its exceptional tracking abilities, originating from 18th-century crosses between Bloodhounds and Foxhounds. Standing **23–27 inches tall** (males 25–27", females 23–25") and weighing **65–90 lbs**, this breed boasts a **black-and-tan coat**, medium energy, and a **powerful, independent nose** optimized for long-distance trail tracking of raccoons, deer, and even bears. Renowned for endurance and adaptability—excelling in both solo and pack hunting—they thrive in rugged terrain. Their outgoing, playful temperament demands **daily moderate exercise** and a secure environment (fenced preferred). While versatile in activities (hunting, agility, search/rescue), their vocal nature and medium shedding make them best suited for active owners. Historically bred for self-reliance, they remain a prized trail companion.
