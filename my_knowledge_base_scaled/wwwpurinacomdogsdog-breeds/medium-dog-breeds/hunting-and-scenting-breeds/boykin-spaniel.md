# Boykin Spaniel

The **Boykin Spaniel** is a medium-sized, high-energy hunting breed known for its cheerful temperament, affectionate nature, and strong bond with people and other animals. Originating in South Carolina, this breed was developed by L. Whitaker Boykin from stray dogs and excels as a retriever for upland game and waterfowl, including turkey hunting. Males stand **15.5–18 inches** tall and weigh **25–35 lbs**, while females are slightly smaller (**14–16.5 inches**). Their **medium-length, feathered coat** (flat to wavy) comes in rich liver, brown, or dark chocolate and requires regular grooming to avoid matting. As an active, intelligent breed, Boykins thrive in families that provide ample exercise, mental stimulation, and participation in activities like hunting, agility, obedience, and field trials. Officially recognized as South Carolina’s state dog, this breed combines versatility, loyalty, and adaptability, making it ideal for outdoor enthusiasts and families seeking a devoted companion.
