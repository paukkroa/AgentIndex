This section explores **five of the world’s largest dog breeds**, each weighing over 100 pounds, highlighting their distinct origins, temperaments, and historical roles. From the **Great Pyrenees**, a majestic guardian bred for livestock protection in the Pyrenees Mountains, to the **Irish Wolfhound**, a towering hunter with a regal presence, and the **Newfoundland**, a gentle, water-loving family companion, these breeds showcase diversity in size, purpose, and personality while emphasizing their unique contributions as working dogs and beloved pets.
