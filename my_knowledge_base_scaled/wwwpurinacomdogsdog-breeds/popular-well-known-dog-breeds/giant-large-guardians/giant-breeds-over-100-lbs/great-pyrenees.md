# Great Pyrenees

The **Great Pyrenees** is a **giant, independent, and loyal** breed originating from the Pyrenees Mountains of southwestern Europe, historically bred as **livestock guardians and watchdogs**. Standing **25–32 inches tall** and weighing **85–130 lbs**, this **medium-energy** breed features a **thick, double-coated** appearance, primarily in **white with gray/badger/tan markings**. Known for their **mellow yet intelligent** temperament, they excel in **herding, guarding, therapy work, and conformation shows** but may resist traditional obedience training due to their **independent nature**. Early **socialization and training** are critical to manage their **territorial instincts**. Their **heavy shedding** requires **weekly brushing**, though their weather-resistant coat needs minimal grooming. Health concerns include **bloat, hip/elbow dysplasia, and eye disorders**, mitigated by **responsible breeding practices**. Historically revered by **French royalty** and introduced to the U.S. in **1824**, this ancient breed thrives on **moderate exercise** and benefits from **large-breed-specific nutrition** for optimal growth and health.
