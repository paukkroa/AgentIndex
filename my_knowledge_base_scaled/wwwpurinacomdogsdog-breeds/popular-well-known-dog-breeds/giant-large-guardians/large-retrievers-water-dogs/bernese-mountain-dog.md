# Bernese Mountain Dog

The **Bernese Mountain Dog**, or "Berner," is a massive yet gentle working breed originating from Swiss farms, historically used for herding, drafting, and carting. Standing **23–27.5 inches tall** and weighing **70–115 lbs**, Berners possess a **calm, affectionate temperament**, thriving as family companions despite their low energy. Their **tri-color coat** (black, white, and rust) requires **daily brushing** due to heavy shedding, especially during spring. Known for their **intelligence and eagerness to please**, they respond well to obedience training but need **moderate exercise** (walks/hikes) to stay healthy. Health risks include **bloat, hip dysplasia, cancer, and blood disorders**, necessitating **responsible breeding practices**. Originally nearly extinct, their revival began in **1907** under Professor Albert Heim’s efforts, with AKC recognition in **1937**. Berners excel in **drafting** (pulling heavy loads) and are part of the **Swiss Mountain Dog family**, distinguished by their **long, silky coat**.
