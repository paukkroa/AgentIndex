# Giant Schnauzer

The **Giant Schnauzer** is a large, robust working breed (25.5–27.5" males, 55–85 lbs) with a wiry, low-shedding double coat in black or salt-and-pepper. Bred in the 19th century as a livestock-driving and guard dog, this intelligent, high-energy breed excels in police/military work, agility, obedience, and Schutzhund. Known for its distinctive beard and eyebrows, the Giant Schnauzer is fiercely loyal, protective, and requires **daily vigorous exercise**. With a lifespan of **12–15 years**, health concerns include hip dysplasia and thyroid issues, mitigated by responsible breeding. Its dense coat demands **weekly brushing and regular clipping/stripping**. Ideal for active owners, it thrives on purpose-driven tasks and benefits from **large-breed puppy food** (first year) and **weight-conscious adult formulas** if needed.
