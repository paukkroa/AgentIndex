# Belgian Malinois

The **Belgian Malinois** is a medium-sized, high-energy herding breed originating from Malines, Belgium, renowned for its alert, confident, and protective yet affectionate temperament. Standing at **20–22 inches** tall and weighing **40–75 lbs**, this breed features a **short fawn-to-mahogany coat** with black-tipped hairs, often called a "brindle" appearance. Developed in the 1800s for herding and working roles, the Malinois excels in **police/military work, search/rescue, agility, obedience, and Schutzhund**—demonstrating intelligence, trainability, and endurance. Historically underrepresented in the U.S. post-WWII, its popularity surged in the 1960s due to strategic imports. Ideal for active owners, this breed thrives in structured environments requiring mental and physical stimulation, though its protective instincts necessitate early socialization.
