# Belgian Sheepdog

The **Belgian Sheepdog** is a large, elegant, and highly intelligent herding breed originating in 19th-century Belgium, known for its muscular build, dense black double coat (requiring weekly brushing), and high energy. Standing **22–26 inches tall** and weighing **45–65 lbs**, this active breed thrives in purpose-driven roles, excelling in **agility, obedience, herding, search/rescue, police work, and therapy**. Descended from the four Belgian sheepdog types, it traces lineage to **Nicholas Rose’s Groenendael kennel**, where its ancestors served as **message carriers and ambulance dogs during World Wars**. Ideal for experienced owners, the Belgian Sheepdog demands mental stimulation and physical activity to prevent boredom.
