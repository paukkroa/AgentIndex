# Belgian Tervuren

The **Belgian Tervuren** is a large, high-energy herding and guard dog breed, one of four Belgian sheepdog varieties, known for its intelligent, protective, and affectionate temperament. Males stand **24–26 inches** (60–75 lbs) and females **22–24 inches** (40–55 lbs), with a **medium-to-long fawn-to-russet mahogany coat** overlaid with black. Bred for versatility, this breed excels in **therapy work, agility, herding, obedience, and search/rescue**, requiring **daily mental/physical stimulation**. Their **double coat** demands **weekly brushing** to prevent matting. Originating from the Belgian village of Tervuren (home to early breeder M.F. Corbeel), the breed gained recognition in 1907 with champion **Milsart**. Ideal for active owners, the Tervuren thrives as a **devoted companion** but needs firm, consistent training due to its strong protective instincts.
