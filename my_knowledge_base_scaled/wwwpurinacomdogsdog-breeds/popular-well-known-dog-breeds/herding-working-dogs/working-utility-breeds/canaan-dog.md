# Canaan Dog

The **Canaan Dog**, an ancient breed with roots in Biblical times, is a medium-sized, short-coated working dog originally bred for herding and guarding in the Land of Canaan. Males stand **20–24 inches** (45–55 lbs) and females **19–23 inches** (35–45 lbs), with a **harsh double coat** offering protection against extreme climates while requiring minimal grooming. Known for their **loyalty to a single family or person**, they exhibit a **territorial and suspicious nature toward strangers**, making them excellent guardians. Historically, they served in **police, military, and mine detection roles** (e.g., WWII Middle East forces) and excel in **agility, obedience, and conformation competitions**. Their **medium energy** and adaptability suit active households, though their strong bond with one primary caregiver is key. The breed’s distinctive **white coat with a mask** (or solid shades of black/brown) and rare U.S. lineage (established in 1965) further distinguish them.
