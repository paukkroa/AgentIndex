# Portuguese Water Dog

The **Portuguese Water Dog** (*Cão de Água*), a medium-sized, athletic breed (males: 20–23" / 42–60 lbs; females: 17–21" / 35–50 lbs), is a historically working dog bred in Portugal for maritime tasks, including retrieving, diving, and guarding fishermen. Its **waterproof, hypoallergenic coat** (black, white, brown, or yellow) and **webbed feet** make it an exceptional swimmer, while its **medium energy level** demands daily exercise and mental stimulation. Known for **obedience, intelligence, and affection**, this breed excels in activities like agility, obedience, and water rescue. Requiring **regular grooming** despite minimal shedding, the Portuguese Water Dog thrives in active households and is ideal for allergy sufferers due to its low-dander coat.
