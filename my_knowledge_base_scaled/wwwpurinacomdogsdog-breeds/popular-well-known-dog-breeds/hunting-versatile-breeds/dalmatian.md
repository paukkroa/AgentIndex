# Dalmatian

The **Dalmatian** is a medium-to-large, high-energy breed (19–24" tall, 45–70 lbs) known for its iconic **white coat with black or liver spots**, born pure white before spots develop at 7–10 days. Originally bred as **coach dogs** to guard horses and carriages, this intelligent, muscular breed retains a strong work ethic and watchdog instinct. With **high stamina**, they excel in agility, obedience, and tracking, thriving in active households. Their **short coat** sheds moderately but requires weekly brushing and occasional baths. While generally healthy with a lifespan of **10–13 years**, they may be prone to **deafness and kidney stones**, mitigated by responsible breeding. Historically linked to fire engines and firehouse mascots, Dalmatians remain loyal, affectionate companions, ideal for runners, hikers, or families. Unique spot patterns and historical ties to Romani caravans and British nobility further distinguish this breed.
