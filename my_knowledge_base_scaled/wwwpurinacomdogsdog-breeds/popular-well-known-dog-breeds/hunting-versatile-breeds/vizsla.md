# Vizsla

The **Vizsla** is a medium-sized, high-energy Hungarian sporting breed known for its athletic build, golden-rust coat, and deep bond with humans. Standing **21–24 inches** tall and weighing **44–60 lbs**, Vizslas thrive on **daily physical and mental stimulation**, excelling in agility, obedience, and field trials. Their short, low-maintenance coat requires minimal grooming, though seasonal shedding occurs. Originally bred by the Magyar people for hunting, Vizslas are intelligent, sensitive, and prone to separation anxiety, making them ideal for active families. Historically nearly extinct in the late 1800s, they were reintroduced to the U.S. in 1950 and later became the first AKC dog to earn championships in five sports. Health-wise, they are generally robust but may develop allergies, hip dysplasia, or epilepsy. A high-protein diet supports their active lifestyle, while puppies need nutrient-rich puppy food for growth.
