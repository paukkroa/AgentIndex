# Wirehaired Vizsla

The **Wirehaired Vizsla** is a medium-sized, high-energy hunting breed developed in the 1930s by Hungarian hunters to thrive in harsh northern climates. Standing **21.5–25 inches tall** (females 21.5–23", males 23–25") and weighing **40–60 lbs**, this athletic dog features a **short, wiry golden-rust coat** requiring regular brushing and occasional bathing. Known for its **loyal, gentle, and trainable temperament**, the Wirehaired Vizsla excels in **hunting, agility, obedience, tracking, and search/rescue**, needing vigorous exercise and mental stimulation to prevent boredom. Officially recognized by the **AKC in 2014**, this people-oriented breed was first introduced to North America in the **1970s**. Ideal for active owners, it thrives in family activities but may struggle in sedentary environments.
