# Standard Schnauzer

The **Standard Schnauzer** is a medium-sized, high-energy German farm dog originally bred for livestock protection and vermin control, known for its wiry, low-shedding coat and distinctive bearded muzzle. Standing **17–20 inches tall** (males 18–20", females 17–19") and weighing **35–45 lbs**, this territorial breed excels in **agility, obedience, herding, and search/rescue work**. Its **black or salt-and-pepper coat** requires regular brushing and **annual stripping** to maintain texture. Energetic and intelligent, the Standard Schnauzer thrives on daily exercise and mental stimulation, making it a loyal yet protective companion. As the oldest Schnauzer breed (derived from *"schnauze"* meaning "muzzle"), it shares traits with Miniature and Giant Schnauzers but retains a distinct working-dog temperament.
