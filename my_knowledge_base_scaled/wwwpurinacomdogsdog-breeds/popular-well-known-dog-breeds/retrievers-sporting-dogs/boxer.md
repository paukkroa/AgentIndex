# Boxer

The **Boxer** is a medium-to-large, high-energy breed (males: 23–24" / 60–80 lbs; females: 21.5–23.5" / 55–65 lbs) with a short coat, athletic build, and expressive wrinkled forehead. Originally bred in 19th-century Germany from the Bullenbeisser (a large-game hunter), Boxers transitioned from fighting/hunting to becoming loyal, protective family companions. Known for their intelligence, problem-solving skills, and spirited temperament, they require **daily exercise** (walks, agility, or play) and **early socialization** to curb stubbornness. While patient with children, they are wary of strangers and fearless when threatened. Health concerns include **hip dysplasia, heart conditions, thyroid issues, and cancers**; responsible breeders screen for these. Boxers thrive on **high-protein diets** (adults) and balanced puppy food. Their lifespan is **10–12 years**, and they tolerate neither extreme heat nor cold. Historically, they were among the first breeds trained for police work and are related to bulldogs.
