# Chinook

The **Chinook** is a rare, medium-energy, large working breed developed in the early 1900s by Arthur Treadwell Walden in New Hampshire, blending Mastiff, Greenland Husky, and German/Belgian Shepherd genetics. Known for its speed, endurance, and athleticism, this breed excels in sledding, carting, agility, obedience, and endurance sports like skijoring and backpacking. Males stand **24–26 inches** (60–75 lbs) and females **22–24 inches** (50–65 lbs), with a medium-length, low-maintenance tawny coat. Historically significant, the breed’s name honors a sled dog that supported Admiral Byrd’s Antarctic expedition, and its record-breaking 502-mile trek in 1941 cemented its legacy. Once the rarest breed (peaking at 125 in 1965), the Chinook remains a unique American creation, prized for its versatility and adaptability.
