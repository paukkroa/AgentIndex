# Golden Retriever

The **Golden Retriever** is a large, medium-coated, medium-energy breed renowned for its intelligence, loyalty, and adaptability, making it one of the most beloved family and working dogs in the U.S. Standing **21.5–24 inches tall** and weighing **55–75 lbs**, Goldens thrive in diverse roles—from **hunting, agility, and search-and-rescue** to **therapy, service, and companion work**. Their **eager-to-please temperament** and trainability stem from their Sporting Group heritage, requiring **regular exercise** to prevent boredom or behavioral issues. With a **lustrous golden coat** (ranging from light to dark) and a **double-layered, water-repellent fur**, they shed heavily seasonally and moderately year-round, necessitating **weekly brushing**. Health-wise, they are generally robust but prone to **hip/elbow dysplasia, heart conditions, and ear infections**, so proactive screening and ear care are advised.
