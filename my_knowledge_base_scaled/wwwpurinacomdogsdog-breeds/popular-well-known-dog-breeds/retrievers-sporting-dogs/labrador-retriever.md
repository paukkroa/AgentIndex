# Labrador Retriever

The **Labrador Retriever** is a large, high-energy breed known for its friendly, intelligent, and outgoing temperament, earning its reputation as America’s favorite dog. Standing **21.5–24.5 inches** tall and weighing **55–80 lbs**, Labs possess a **short, dense double coat** in standard colors—**black, yellow, or chocolate**—with moderate to heavy shedding requiring regular brushing. Their athletic build and love for water make them exceptional **swimmers, retrievers, and working dogs**, excelling in roles like **search-and-rescue, service work, and detection tasks**. Early socialization and training are critical due to their high energy, as boredom can lead to destructive behaviors. Health-wise, Labs are prone to **obesity, hip/elbow dysplasia, heart disorders, and eye conditions**, necessitating a **balanced diet, large-breed-specific nutrition, and reputable breeding practices**. Their lifespan averages **10–12 years**, and their historical roots trace back to **Newfoundland (originally "St. John’s dogs")**, later refined in England as hunting retrievers before gaining global popularity post-WWII.
