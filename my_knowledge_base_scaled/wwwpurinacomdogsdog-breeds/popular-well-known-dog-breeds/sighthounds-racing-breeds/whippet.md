# Whippet

The **Whippet** is a medium-sized, elegant sighthound breed developed in England for racing and rabbit hunting, known for its streamlined physique, speed (up to **35 mph**), and gentle temperament. Standing **18–22 inches tall** and weighing **25–40 lbs**, Whippets feature a short, smooth coat in any color, minimal shedding, and a dignified yet affectionate personality. They thrive on moderate exercise—ideal for fenced yards or apartment living—while requiring a **healthy weight diet** (e.g., high-quality adult or weight-management food) to support their lean frame. Historically bred from Greyhounds by working-class miners, Whippets gained AKC recognition in **1888** and later became cultural icons, including the first flying disc dog. Their unique **double suspension gallop** (airborne twice per stride) enhances their speed, while their calm demeanor makes them excellent family pets with a lifespan of **12–15 years**.
