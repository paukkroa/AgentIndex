# Caucasian Shepherd Dog

The **Caucasian Shepherd Dog** is an ancient, extra-large breed (99–170 lbs) originating from the Caucasus Mountains, where it was bred as a formidable guardian. Known for its **double-coated, coarse fur** and **medium energy**, this breed exhibits two distinct types: the **Mountain** (longer coat, heavier build) and **Steppe** (shorter coat, lighter frame). With a **regal presence**, they are intelligent but strong-willed, requiring **early socialization and training** to curb territorial instincts. Their **color palette** ranges widely (cream, fawn, black, brindle, etc.), excluding solid black. Originally introduced to the U.S. in the **1970s**, they thrive on **high-protein diets** (2,400+ daily calories for adults) and demand **structured feeding** to prevent joint issues. Ideal for experienced owners, they balance loyalty with independence, making them exceptional protectors but demanding companions.
