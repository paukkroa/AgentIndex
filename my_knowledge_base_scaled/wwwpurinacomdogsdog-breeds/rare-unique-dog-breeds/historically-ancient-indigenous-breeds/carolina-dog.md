# Carolina Dog

The **Carolina Dog**, also known as the **American Dingo**, is a rare, medium-sized sighthound with a distinctive fox-like appearance, including a curved tail, pointed ears, and a fox-like nose. Originating from Asia via the Bering Land Bridge with Paleo-Indians, this regional breed thrives in group settings and is primarily found along the Georgia-South Carolina border. Known for their loyalty to their families while being reserved with strangers, they possess a medium energy level and a unique coat—short and smooth on the head, ears, and front legs, with longer, coarse hair on the neck, withers, and back. Colors range from ginger tones with white markings to solid black or piebald, often featuring "angel wings" (lighter fur behind the shoulders). Care involves balanced nutrition (lean protein, vitamins, and minerals), scheduled feeding, and potential joint supplements for conditions like dysplasia. Regular veterinary care, including vaccinations and parasite prevention, is essential for maintaining their health.
