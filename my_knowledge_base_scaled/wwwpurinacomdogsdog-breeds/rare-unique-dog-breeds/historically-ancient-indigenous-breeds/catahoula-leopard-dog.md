# Catahoula Leopard Dog

The **Catahoula Leopard Dog**, a large, muscular, and highly adaptable breed originating in Louisiana, descends from Native American dogs crossbred with European breeds like Beaucerons, Bloodhounds, Mastiffs, and Greyhounds in the late 1700s–early 1800s. Known for their **high energy, intelligence, and versatility**, they excel as farmworkers, particularly in wild boar management, and possess a **distinctive short-to-medium coat** in varied colors (blue, black, brindle, red, etc.), often with **heterochromatic eyes (two different eye colors)** or white markings. As the **state dog of Louisiana**, they thrive on structured routines, requiring **high-protein, balanced diets** (e.g., Purina Pro Plan Sport or Large Breed formulas) and **consistent feeding schedules** to sustain their active lifestyle. Their care includes **moderate grooming** (low-maintenance coat) and **joint/skin supplements** (e.g., omega-3s) to support their robust build.
