# Cirneco dell’Etna

The **Cirneco dell’Etna** is an ancient Sicilian breed with roots tracing back **2,500 years** to Egypt, later introduced to Sicily by the Phoenicians. Descended from similar lineages as the Pharaoh Hound, this medium-sized dog (females: **16.5–19"**, males: **18–19.5"**, weighing **20–23 lbs**) thrives as a versatile hunting and companion breed. Its **short, low-maintenance coat** (tan or chestnut shades) requires minimal grooming. Known for **medium energy**, intelligence, and curiosity, the Cirneco excels in **hunting, agility, obedience, and conformation**, demanding daily physical and mental stimulation. Affectionate yet independent, it adapts well to indoor living. Named after **Mount Etna**, the breed embodies resilience and adaptability, making it a unique blend of historical heritage and modern versatility.
