# Xoloitzcuintli

The **Xoloitzcuintli**, an ancient Mexican breed rooted in Aztec mythology (named after the Aztec god *Xolotl*), is a lean, muscular companion dog available in three sizes—**Toy (10–14" / 5–15 lbs), Miniature (14–18" / 15–30 lbs), and Standard (18–23" / 25–40 lbs)**—with **short-haired or hairless varieties**. Recognized as Mexico’s national treasure, this breed’s origins trace back to guarding homes from evil spirits, though today it thrives as a **calm, alert watchdog and family pet**. Moderate energy levels demand daily exercise, while grooming needs vary by coat type. Known for its striking colors (black, brindle, fawn, gray, etc.) and historical significance (depicted in Rivera/Kahlo’s art), the Xoloitzcuintli excels in **conformation, obedience, agility, and therapy work**.
