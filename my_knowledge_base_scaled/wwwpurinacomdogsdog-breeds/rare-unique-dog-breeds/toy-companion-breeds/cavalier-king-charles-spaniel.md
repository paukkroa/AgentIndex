# Cavalier King Charles Spaniel

The **Cavalier King Charles Spaniel** is a small, affectionate companion breed (12–13" tall, 13–18 lbs) known for its expressive round eyes, feathered coat, and adaptable temperament. Descended from hunting spaniels and later crossbred with Asian toy breeds (e.g., Pugs) in the 19th century, this breed nearly went extinct before being revived in the 1920s. Cavaliers thrive on human interaction, excelling in obedience training and adapting to both active and sedentary lifestyles. Their medium energy makes them ideal for indoor living, though they enjoy outdoor play. Key health concerns include obesity, heart conditions, eye disorders (e.g., cataracts), and joint issues. Lifespan averages **12–15 years**. Four distinct coat colors exist: Blenheim (chestnut markings), black/tan, tricolor, and ruby. Regular grooming prevents matting, while a **small-breed-specific diet** supports their health. Historically tied to British royalty, Cavaliers gained modern fame via *Sex and the City*.
