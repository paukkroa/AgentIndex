# Cesky Terrier

The **Cesky Terrier** is a small, medium-energy, medium-coated breed originating from the Czech Republic, developed by geneticist Frantisek Horák as a pack-hunting companion. Standing **10–13 inches tall** and weighing **16–22 lbs**, this well-muscled breed features a **platinum-to-charcoal gray coat** (often with white, brown, or black markings) and a **reserved yet loyal temperament**, requiring **moderate socialization** to ease interactions with strangers. While not hyperactive like other terriers, they retain a **keen, alert nature** and may chase prey. Their **medium energy** demands regular exercise, and their **fine coat** needs **monthly grooming** with occasional clipping. Ideal for **agility, obedience, and tracking**, they thrive as **low-shedding, family-oriented pets** suited for active households.
